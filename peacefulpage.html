The Peaceful Palm Project

The Problem

Beginning Stages

The Peaceful Palm is a mobile massage therapy business ran by Kai & Cherokee that is looking to revamp their current website. They are looking for expanded expression within their content & hoping for higher traffic volume. Our team worked with them to bring together an updated styled website that is easier to navigate & showcases their passion for helping other’s heal.

Research

Client Kick-Off

To kick off the design project, we began by meeting with Kai & Cherokee to establish some base expectations. From this meeting, we were able to gain an understanding of their overall wants towards design completion: a website that would not only be more informative & expressive, but have a higher traffic rate. They also wanted to emphasize that they are a black-owned business & to capture the trustworthy nature of Kai for his mobile massage clients.

Survey

One of the first methods of gaining data was collected through user surveys- the results giving a better scope of the target user’s massage preferences & frequency. Other user personality traits were also further understood, such as colors associated with relaxation & peacefulness.

Interviews & Insights

Apart from survey responses, we also conducted 7 user interviews ranging between the ages 24-44.

These interviews further touched upon our previous insights, with our responses showing us common trends, high lighting pain points & providing a deeper glimpse into realities of the Peaceful Palm’s potential clients.

Design Process

User Persona

Gaining a deeper understanding of our users allowed us to create a user persona. We allowed this persona to represent what we felt our most common target audience might feel, think & perceive The Peaceful Palm.

Moodboard

I then pieced together various photographs, styles & colors to create a mood board that would showcase The Peaceful Palm’s overarching aesthetic.

Sketches

From here, we began to brainstorm new template ideas & a structure to build upon. In these sketches, we included a potential hamburger redesign towards a pop-out menu.
